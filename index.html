Reinventing fashion

Reinventing the business of fashion: why we need new recipes for the industry

Despite years of investment in sustainability, the fashion and retail value chain is brittle and ill prepared to address the major existential problems facing our planet. The covid crisis has made this very clear. Making sustainable progress will require a concerted effort and unconventional approaches.
That's why in May 2021, Helena Helmerson (CEO of H&M) gave us the challenge to find out what it takes to reinvent the fashion industry, form new partnerships, and reimagine the entire value chain.
The beginning of an exciting, challenging and unpredictable journey.

02

Innovation process

2.1 Recruiting the chefs

With direction from Helena, we set out to identify partners to help in this journey. To describe our process, we developed the metaphor of a kitchen: How can we cook new dishes with different ingredients? Which chefs do we need for this? And what kind of mindset do they need to bring with them to create something new and tasty?
Eventually, we found seven brave companies that were not only willing to send us their chefs, but also to fund the kitchen space and ingredients.

02

Innovation process

2.2 How to create a new menu?

Even though cooking is manual work, it takes preparation to define a common direction. In the Core Team, an innovation strategy was developed and the basis for a trusting collaboration was laid. Before diving into content, we took time to understand who was at in the kitchen and what they brought. We asked each cook to describe their company's strategic intent, their company's unique “superpowers” and their own personal motivation. This created a common understanding of our shared strategic intent and our complimentary skill sets.
Now we were ready to start busting the paradigms that were holding the industry back. A textbook approach to innovation might start with research and a detailed scan of trends in the sector. With this group of senior executives, we fast-forwarded straight to their insights about the ways of working in the industry that once worked but that now were holding us back.

Innovation process

2.3 Welcome to the Kitchen!

Innovation in business is just like developing new recipes. You combine different ingredients and cooking styles to find both complementarity and novelty. The process involves trial and error. Some routes lead to dead ends, others to exciting new discoveries. The process is messy but can be managed with a disciplined approach.
It's remarkable that we've been able to bring our chefs together for an extended period of time, thanks to a combination of technology and the new mentality of online and remote work, normalized by the Covid pandemic.
Very soon, our virtual kitchen in Miro became a lively experimentation field where the teams documented their work progress and met regularly.

3.1 Busting Paradigms

We started with a shortlist of paradigms that are holding the industry back. Ways of working that were once logical and helpful, but now prevent the industry from becoming more resilient and sustainable. We initially agreed on five key paradigms, later combining them to develop a final four. With this starting point set, the teams began to describe an ideal more resilient and sustainable fashion world.

3.2 Data & Consumer - The redeployed design teams

"Fail often, fail fast" is a common saying in the start-up world, but it can also apply to cooking. While two of our design teams were able to make progress fast, we quickly realized that the other two were not quite as productive as standalone streams. Nonetheless, their work provided valuable insights for the overall project and resources were redeployed in an agile manner.
